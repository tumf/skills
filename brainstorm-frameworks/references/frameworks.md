# Brainstorming Framework Catalog

Use this file to choose and apply the right ideation framework.

## 1. SCAMPER

Best for:
- fast divergence
- product and service idea expansion
- forced perspective shifts

Prompts:
- Substitute: What can be replaced?
- Combine: What can be merged?
- Adapt: What can be borrowed from elsewhere?
- Modify: What can be changed or exaggerated?
- Put to other uses: Where else can this work?
- Eliminate: What can be removed?
- Reverse: What if we invert the logic?

Good when:
- the user has one existing idea and wants variations fast
- the session needs mechanical expansion

## 2. Mind Map

Best for:
- solo ideation
- associative thinking
- exploring adjacent branches quickly

Method:
- put the core theme in the center
- branch by audience, problem, channel, feature, risk, or metaphor
- keep expanding without judging early

Good when:
- the user is thinking alone
- the space is still fuzzy
- speed matters more than rigor

## 3. Six Thinking Hats

Best for:
- group discussion
- separating modes of thinking
- stabilizing meeting quality

Modes:
- White: facts and unknowns
- Red: feelings and intuition
- Black: risks and failure modes
- Yellow: benefits and upside
- Green: creative alternatives
- Blue: process control and synthesis

Good when:
- discussion is chaotic
- one loud mode dominates the room
- the user wants a facilitation structure

## 4. KJ Method / Affinity Diagram

Best for:
- clustering many rough ideas
- turning brainstorm output into structure
- UX and planning work

Method:
- list ideas as separate notes
- group by natural affinity
- name each cluster
- derive themes and decisions

Good when:
- the team already has many raw ideas
- synthesis matters more than further divergence

## 5. Brainwriting (6-3-5)

Best for:
- group ideation with less speaking bias
- collecting many options quickly
- avoiding dominance by extroverts or senior voices

Method:
- 6 people
- 3 ideas each
- 5 minutes per round
- pass ideas and build on them

Good when:
- verbal brainstorming is biased or uneven
- the user wants meeting mechanics

## 6. Random Stimulus

Best for:
- breaking local maxima
- forcing novelty
- AI-assisted ideation

Method:
- generate a random word, object, concept, or scene
- force a connection to the theme
- extract at least one usable pattern

Good when:
- normal ideas all sound the same
- the user wants surprise or originality

## 7. Why / How Ladder

Best for:
- moving between abstract purpose and concrete implementation
- clarifying goals
- avoiding shallow solutioning

Method:
- go upward with Why to reach intent and essence
- go downward with How to reach actions and implementation

Good when:
- the theme is unclear
- the team is stuck between vision and execution

## 8. JTBD

Best for:
- customer-centered product thinking
- reframing features around jobs
- business relevance

Core question:
- What job is the customer hiring this product to do?

Good when:
- feature ideas need user value grounding
- the brainstorm is drifting into solution-first thinking

## 9. ERRC / Blue Ocean

Best for:
- business model and category redesign
- strategic differentiation
- simplifying or repositioning an offer

Prompts:
- Eliminate
- Reduce
- Raise
- Create

Good when:
- the user wants strategy, not just features
- the problem is market positioning or offer design

## 10. Design Sprint

Best for:
- moving from ideas to decisions and validation
- practical product work
- structured team progress

Pattern:
- diverge
- converge
- prototype
- test

Good when:
- the user wants an end-to-end flow
- ideation must lead to validation, not just a list
