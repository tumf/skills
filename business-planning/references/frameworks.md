# Business Planning Framework Catalog

This reference is a concise catalog of the main frameworks supported by the skill.
Use it when you need a reminder of what each framework is for and what output it should produce.

## SWOT
Purpose:
- summarize internal strengths and weaknesses with external opportunities and threats

Use when:
- you already have some market understanding
- you need to align strategy around current position

Output should produce:
- 2 to 5 items for each quadrant
- strategic implications
- what to strengthen, fix, capture, or defend against

## 3C
Purpose:
- understand Customer, Company, Competitor together

Use when:
- you need a grounded picture of market reality
- you are choosing where to play and what edge you may have

Output should produce:
- target customer profile and need
- company capability snapshot
- competitor landscape and gaps
- implications for positioning

## PEST
Purpose:
- scan external forces: Political, Economic, Social, Technological

Use when:
- regulation, macro trends, or technology shifts could reshape the business

Output should produce:
- major external drivers
- risks and enablers
- assumptions that must be monitored over time

## Business Model Canvas
Purpose:
- map the whole business model in nine blocks

Use when:
- the business needs a broad operating-model view
- partnerships, channels, and resources matter materially

Output should produce:
- customer segments
- value propositions
- channels
- customer relationships
- revenue streams
- key resources
- key activities
- key partners
- cost structure

## Lean Canvas
Purpose:
- structure a startup hypothesis quickly

Use when:
- the business is early-stage and uncertain
- the team needs a compact model to test

Output should produce:
- problem
- customer segments
- unique value proposition
- solution
- channels
- revenue streams
- cost structure
- key metrics
- unfair advantage

## 5 Forces
Purpose:
- analyze industry attractiveness and structural pressure

Use when:
- competitive intensity, supplier power, buyer power, substitutes, or entry barriers matter

Output should produce:
- force-by-force assessment
- where profit is likely to leak
- implications for go-to-market and differentiation

## STP
Purpose:
- choose who to serve and how to position

Use when:
- the market is broad and the team needs focus
- messaging and differentiation matter

Output should produce:
- segmentation options
- target segment choice
- positioning statement
- what segments to avoid for now

## Ansoff Matrix
Purpose:
- compare growth options by market and product direction

Use when:
- the business already has some base to expand from

Output should produce:
- market penetration options
- market development options
- product development options
- diversification options
- risk and capability comparison

## KGI / KPI
Purpose:
- turn strategy into measurable execution

Use when:
- the plan needs operating discipline

Output should produce:
- KGI definition
- 3 to 7 leading KPI
- cadence and threshold logic if possible

## Unit Economics
Purpose:
- test whether one customer or account is economically attractive

Use when:
- LTV, CAC, payback, gross margin, or retention drive viability

Output should produce:
- formula definitions
- rough assumptions or required inputs
- break-even and scaling implications

## Lean Startup
Purpose:
- validate critical assumptions through build-measure-learn loops

Use when:
- uncertainty is high and the next best move is an experiment

Output should produce:
- core hypotheses
- MVP or pilot scope
- success metrics
- what to learn before scaling

## Design Thinking
Purpose:
- discover and frame customer problems from empathy first

Use when:
- user pain is unclear
- workflow or product experience matters more than top-down strategy

Output should produce:
- user insight summary
- problem definition
- possible solution directions
- prototype or test ideas
