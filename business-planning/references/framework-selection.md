# Framework Selection for Business Planning

Use this reference when deciding which business-planning frameworks to apply.

The main rule is simple:
choose the framework by decision need, not by familiarity.

## Fast selection table

### Need macro context or regulatory risk
Use:
- PEST

Best for:
- new market entry
- policy-sensitive sectors
- AI, finance, healthcare, public sector
- long-horizon planning

### Need a market reality check
Use:
- 3C

Best for:
- understanding customers, your company, and competitors together
- sharpening the real playing field
- most startup and SaaS planning work

### Need an integrated strategic snapshot
Use:
- SWOT

Best for:
- pulling internal and external findings into one summary
- discussing strategic options with a team
- growth-stage business reviews

### Need a hypothesis-driven startup plan
Use:
- Lean Canvas

Best for:
- 0→1 ideas
- uncertain products
- early-stage AI agent and SaaS concepts
- MVP-first planning

### Need a broader operating model view
Use:
- Business Model Canvas

Best for:
- partnership-heavy models
- multi-sided businesses
- operational design beyond the product itself

### Need industry structure and margin pressure
Use:
- 5 Forces

Best for:
- crowded markets
- commoditizing segments
- sectors where bargaining power matters

### Need target segment and differentiation
Use:
- STP

Best for:
- go-to-market planning
- category positioning
- deciding what segment not to chase

### Need growth path options
Use:
- Ansoff matrix

Best for:
- deciding whether to expand by market, product, or diversification
- post-PMF planning

### Need numerical execution control
Use:
- KGI / KPI

Best for:
- turning strategy into operating cadence
- aligning team actions with targets

### Need basic economic viability
Use:
- Unit economics

Best for:
- SaaS, subscription, marketplace, usage billing
- any business where acquisition cost and retention matter

### Need uncertainty reduction by testing
Use:
- Lean Startup

Best for:
- MVP design
- pilot planning
- staged validation

### Need deeper customer-problem understanding
Use:
- Design Thinking

Best for:
- unclear user pain
- workflow redesign
- when customer empathy is more important than market structure

## Selection heuristics

If the user asks for a complete business plan from scratch:
- default to 3C + Lean Canvas + KPI design
- add PEST for heavy external risk
- add Unit economics for subscription or repeat-purchase businesses

If the user asks whether a business idea is worth pursuing:
- use 3C + Lean Canvas + Lean Startup
- add Design Thinking if the pain is still fuzzy

If the user asks how to beat incumbents:
- use 3C + 5 Forces + STP

If the user asks how to grow an existing business:
- use SWOT + STP + Ansoff + KPI design
- add Unit economics before recommending aggressive growth

If the user asks for an AI agent or infrastructure business plan:
- use 3C + Lean Canvas + STP + Unit economics
- add PEST if regulation, data residency, or vendor concentration matters

## Combination playbooks

### 1. Environment → market reality → model
- PEST → 3C → Lean Canvas
Use when:
- the market is changing fast
- policy or technology shifts matter
- the business is still hypothesis-heavy

### 2. Market reality → segment choice → execution
- 3C → STP → KPI design
Use when:
- the business exists but focus is weak
- the main problem is choosing the right customer and message

### 3. Strategy review → economics → expansion
- SWOT → Unit economics → Ansoff
Use when:
- the team wants growth but may be overestimating readiness

### 4. Customer insight → MVP test plan
- Design Thinking → Lean Canvas → Lean Startup
Use when:
- the customer problem is not yet well-defined
- the next step should be interview, prototype, or pilot

### 5. Structural pressure → positioning
- 5 Forces → STP
Use when:
- margins are weak
- competition is intense
- differentiation must be chosen carefully

## Escalation rules

If the first pass is too abstract:
- add Lean Canvas or Business Model Canvas

If the first pass is too idea-heavy and not testable:
- add Lean Startup and KPI design

If the first pass ignores numbers:
- add Unit economics

If the first pass is strategically neat but not customer-grounded:
- add 3C or Design Thinking

## Output guidance

For every chosen framework, answer three things:
1. What question does it answer?
2. What did we learn?
3. What action changes because of that?

If a framework does not change a decision, it probably was not needed.
